Frida Kahlo's Timeline

Frida Kahlo was born on July 6, 1907 in Coyocoan, Mexico City, Mexico.


She contracted polio at the age of 6 and had to be bedridden for nine months.


Frida Kahlo attended the renowned National Preparatory School in Mexico City in the year of 1922.


Frida Kahlo reconnected with Rivera in 1928. She asked him to evaluate her work and he encouraged her.


In 1932, Kahlo added more realistic and surrealistic components in her painting style. In the painting titled Henry Ford Hospital(1932), Frida Kahlo lied on a hospital bed naked and was surrounded with a few things floating around, which includes a fetus, a flower, a pelvis, a snail, all connected by veins. This painting was an expression of her feelings about her second miscarriage. It is as personal as her other self-portraits.


In 1938, Frida Kahlo became a friend of Andre Breton, who is one of the primary figures of the Surrealism movement. In the same year, she had an exhibition at New York City gallery.


1939, Kahlo was invited by Andre Breton and went to Paris. Her works are exhibited there and she is befriended with artists such as Marc Chagall, Piet Mondrian, and Pablo Picasso.


In the year of 1944, Frida Kahlo painted one of her most famous portraits, The Broken Column.


July 13, 1954 Frida Kahlo dies from depression and other causes. Her work is still appreciated today and Frida Kahlo has become an icon in the world of painting.
